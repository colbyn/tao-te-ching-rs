1.1 The Tao is nameless and unchanging.

1.2 Although it appears insignificant,

1.3 nothing in the world can contain it.

2.1 If a ruler abides by its principles,

2.2 then her people will willingly follow.

2.3 Heaven would then reign on earth,

2.4 like sweet rain falling on paradise.

2.5 People would have no need for laws,

2.6 because the law would be written on their hearts.

3.1 Naming is a necessity for order,

3.2 but naming can not order all things.

3.3 Naming often makes things impersonal,

3.4 so we should know when naming should end.

3.5 Knowing when to stop naming,

3.6 you can avoid the pitfall it brings.

4.1 All things end in the Tao

4.2 just as the small streams and the largest rivers

4.3 flow through valleys to the sea.
